Position Description

The University of California, Berkeley seeks applications for a Project Scientist at the Assistant, Associate, or full rank to help create advanced forecasts of high-impact weather and climate phenomena for deployment at scale in tropical countries.  As part of a multidisciplinary collaboration between universities, international development agencies, and governmental ministries in multiple countries, the selected candidate will contribute to the development of forecasts of dangerous heat stress, precipitation, monsoon onset, and other tropical phenomena using a range of physics-based, artificial intelligence, and statistical weather prediction models.  The candidate will play a central role in Berkeley’s new Tropical Risk Project; this project has grown out of a collaboration with the University of Chicago that, over the past two years, developed new AI-enabled long-lead forecasts of rainy season onset that were distributed to over 50 million farmers in India and subsequently institutionalized in the Indian government. This Project Scientist role will help broaden this program to other phenomena, including heat stress, and support its expansion into other countries. In addition to original scholarly research, the role will involve managing activities of the project and contributing to writing of grant proposals. The position will be supervised by Profs. William Boos (Department of Earth and Planetary Science) and Lucas Vargas Zeppetello (Department of Environmental Science, Policy, and Management).

 

In this position, you will:

  • Develop novel decision-oriented forecasting metrics, develop forecasting systems using AI, statistical, and physics-based methods, and evaluate forecast skill compared to novel baselines tailored for decision-makers.
  • Work as a key member of a fast-paced, multi-institutional team to develop built-for-purpose regional forecasts to improve livelihoods of farmers in low- and middle-income countries.
  • Build fundamental understanding of the forecasting potential for high-impact phenomena, such as extreme heat, using AI and statistical weather models.
  • Collaborate with meteorological agencies in multiple countries to help build an operational system to provide actionable, real-time predictions to tens of millions of people throughout the tropics, leveraging last-mile delivery pipelines developed by non-governmental partner organizations.

We are looking for:

  • Ability to work effectively in a deadline-oriented, collaborative team environment with other scientists, postdoctoral scholars, graduate students, and engineers.
  • Excellent analytical, organizational, and multi-tasking skills.
  • Excellent communication skills to effectively interact with international partners that include scientists, engineers, ministerial officials, and students.
  • Ability to travel domestically and internationally to work with project partners and to present work at collaborative meetings, workshops, and conferences.
  • Capability to be flexible and creative in pursuing new topics and carrying out forefront

research in the area of weather forecasting in the tropics, including with AI and statistical tools.

  • Ability to lead composition of publications, as part of a collaborative research

team that extends across and beyond UC Berkeley, to communicate the technical and scientific achievements of the team to a global audience.
